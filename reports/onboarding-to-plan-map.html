Q5Unit preference

Q6Sex (for calorie accuracy)
male · female — required (gates Continue)
  • The BMR sex coefficient: +5 kcal for male, −161 for female (Mifflin-St Jeor). This is the single biggest physiological lever on your calorie target — a male and female of identical size differ by ~166 kcal/day.
  • If left unknown, an averaged −78 coefficient is used.

Q7Height
120–220 cm — required
  • Adds 6.25 kcal to BMR per cm of height (Mifflin-St Jeor). Taller bodies get a higher calorie target.
Screen3_BodyStats.tsx:195 → tdee_calculator.py:116
Q8Current weight
30–250 kg — required
  • Adds 10 kcal to BMR per kg — the largest single BMR input.
  • Sets your protein target (grams per kg of bodyweight) and the fat floor (≥ 0.5 g/kg for hormonal health).
  • Drives calorie burn per step in the activity science (~40 kcal per 1,000 steps, scaled by weight).
  • Combined with goal weight, feeds the pace sanity gate (blocks dangerously fast targets).
Screen3_BodyStats.tsx:222 → tdee_calculator.py:116,433,440
Q9Goal weight
30–250 kg — required
  • The direction + size of your weight change vs current weight. With your target date it sets the required weekly pace and feeds the pace gate (caution / hard-block for unsafe rates).
Screen3_BodyStats.tsx:249 → Screen4_Goal.tsx:67
Q10Body fat % (optional)
5–50% · or skip
  • Unlocks the Katch-McArdle BMR formula (lean-mass based), more precise than Mifflin for lean/heavy athletes.
  • Protein is then calculated off lean body mass instead of total weight — leaner targeting.
  • High body fat (>25% male / >35% female) lets fat-loss use a steeper 25% deficit instead of 20%.
  • Skipped → falls back to total-weight Mifflin (zero penalty, just less precise).

Screen 1 — You & Goal · Goal

Q11Your goal
fat_loss · muscle_gain · recomp · maintenance · endurance — required
  • Sets the calorie delta off your TDEE: fat loss −20% (or −25% if high body-fat); muscle gain a surplus of +400 / +250 / +150 kcal (beginner→advanced); recomp −200; endurance +200; maintenance 0.
  • Picks your protein & carb ranges (g/kg): fat loss runs protein high at 2.3–2.7 g/kg to protect muscle; muscle gain 1.6–2.2; etc.
  • Sets your rep scheme: fat loss 3×12–15 (RPE 7); muscle/recomp 4×8–12 (RPE 7.5); endurance 2×15–20; maintenance 3×8–12.
  • Sets your daily step ceiling: fat loss 8k–12k; maintenance 7k–10k; muscle gain 6k–8k.

Screen 2 — Training

Q17How intense should training be?
starter · everyday · fit · athlete — required
  • The master dial for your whole workout. Each tier is a fixed spec:
  • Starter → 3 days/wk, 20 min, walk + mobility only (no lifting generated), beginner/bodyweight.
  • Everyday → 5 exercises, 3 days/wk, 30 min, beginner+intermediate.
  • Fit → 6 exercises, 4 days/wk, 45 min, no difficulty cap.
  • Athlete → 8 exercises, 5 days/wk, 60 min, intermediate+advanced, no mandatory rest.
  • The tier also implies your activity-level multiplier for TDEE (sedentary 1.20 → very active 1.725) and your training experience, so you're never asked those separately.
Screen7_5_Intensity.tsx → intensity_tiers.py:31-44 · fast_plan_assembler.py:954

Worked example — every number from real answers

Sample athlete: female, 29, 164 cm, 78 kg → goal 72 kg, fat loss, "fit" intensity, non-veg, lactose-intolerant. Here is exactly what the engine produces.

# Stage 1 — Energy (Mifflin-St Jeor, female) BMR = 10×78 + 6.25×164 − 5×29 − 161 = 780 + 1025 − 145 − 161 = 1499 kcal # "fit" tier ⇒ moderately active ⇒ ×1.55 TDEE = 1499 × 1.55 = 2323 kcal # fat_loss, no body-fat given ⇒ standard −20% Target = 2323 × 0.80 = 1858 kcal (> 1200 floor ✓) # Stage 2 — Macros Protein = 78 × 2.5 g/kg = 195 g (780 kcal) fat-loss runs high to spare muscle Fat = max(1858×27.5%/9, 78×0.5) = 57 g (511 kcal) Carbs = (1858 − 780 − 511) / 4 = 142 g (567 kcal)
Honesty note: at this calorie level the 2.5 g/kg protein target pushes carbs to ~1.8 g/kg, just under the fat-loss carb range — the engine flags this as a soft note (not a block). It is intentional: protein is prioritised to preserve lean mass during the cut.
